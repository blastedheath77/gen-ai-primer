Probability, Not Certainty

The landscape of possibilities

The model doesn't pick one "right answer." It calculates a probability for every possible next token — thousands of candidates, each with a score. The top few are all plausible; the choice between them is where randomness and creativity live.

Key Points

  • Every generation step is a fresh probability distribution over the entire vocabulary
  • The model doesn't "plan ahead" — it only sees up to the current point
  • Picking lower-probability tokens leads to more surprising text
  • This is the mechanical basis for the "creativity vs accuracy" trade-off
