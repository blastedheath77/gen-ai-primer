Patterns from Text

How data becomes knowledge

An LLM's predictions emerge from statistical patterns in vast quantities of text. If "Edinburgh" often follows "capital of Scotland is," the model learns this association — not because someone programmed it, but because the pattern appears in the data.

Key Points

  • No one programmed "Edinburgh is the capital of Scotland" — it learned from patterns
  • More data leads to better predictions
  • Simple pattern matching gets surprisingly far, but real LLMs go much deeper
  • Real LLMs train on a significant fraction of all text ever written
