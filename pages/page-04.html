Patterns from Text

How piles of data turn into something that feels like knowledge.

An LLM's predictions emerge from statistical patterns in vast quantities of text. If "Edinburgh" often follows "capital of Scotland is," the model learns this association — not because someone programmed it, but because the pattern appears in the data.

Key concepts

Learned, not programmed no rulebook
Scale matters more = sharper
Counting goes far, but not far enough shallow patterns
From counts to meaning deeper layers ahead
