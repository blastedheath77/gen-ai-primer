Words as Numbers

Meaning has a shape — and you can measure the distance between ideas.

For a computer to work with words, it converts them into numbers — specifically, a list of numbers called a vector. The key insight: words with similar meanings end up with similar numbers. Happy and joyful are numerically close; happy and forklift are far apart.

Key concepts

Embeddings = vectors [number, number, …]
Similarity is distance close = similar
Relationships as directions king − man + woman
Real space is bigger 768–4,096 dims
Try it
Drag words around on the map. Watch where related words cluster, and try to spot the directions that encode relationships — animal/human, formal/casual, action/object.
