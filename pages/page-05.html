Words as Numbers

Meaning has a shape

For a computer to work with words, it converts them into numbers — specifically, a list of numbers called a vector. The key insight: words with similar meanings end up with similar numbers. "Happy" and "joyful" are numerically close; "happy" and "forklift" are far apart.

Key Points

  • Embeddings are not arbitrary — they encode learned semantic relationships
  • Similar meaning = close in space. This is the foundation of how LLMs "understand"
  • The spatial metaphor extends to relationships between concepts
  • Real embeddings have 768–4096 dimensions, not 2

Words have positions in meaning-space. But a word can mean different things — "bank" could be a riverbank or a financial bank. How does the model know which?
