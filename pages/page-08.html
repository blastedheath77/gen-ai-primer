Layers of Understanding

From surface to meaning

An LLM processes text through many layers — sometimes over a hundred. Early layers detect basic patterns like spelling and grammar. Middle layers grasp meaning and relationships. Deep layers handle nuance, reasoning, and complex inference. Each layer refines the understanding passed to it.

Key Points

  • Real models have 32–128 layers; we show 4 as a simplification
  • No single layer "knows" the answer — it emerges from progressive refinement
  • Bigger models (more layers) tend to be more capable
  • The actual computations are matrix operations
