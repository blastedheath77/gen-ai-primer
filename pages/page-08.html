Layers of Understanding

From surface patterns to deep meaning — one layer at a time.

An LLM processes text through many layers — sometimes over a hundred. No single layer "knows" the answer. Understanding emerges as each layer refines the representations passed to it by the one before.

Key concepts

Early layers: basics spelling, grammar, structure
Middle layers: meaning facts, relationships, entities
Deep layers: nuance inference, irony, abstraction
Emergent, not designed specialisation falls out of training
