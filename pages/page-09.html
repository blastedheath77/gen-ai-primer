Training: Learning at Scale

From random to remarkable

Training is the process of showing the model billions of sentences and adjusting it to predict better each time. It starts knowing nothing — predicting randomly. After trillions of tokens, the patterns it absorbs are so rich that coherent text, answers, and code emerge as consequences of getting better at one thing: prediction.

Key Points

  • Nobody "programs" knowledge into the model — it emerges from exposure to data
  • Training is iterative: millions of small adjustments
  • Scale matters enormously — the jump from 1M to 1B tokens is where real capabilities emerge
  • This is why training is so expensive
