Temperature: The Creativity Dial

Controlling randomness

Temperature controls how the model samples from its probability distribution. Low temperature → almost always picks the most likely token (safe, predictable). High temperature → willing to pick less likely tokens (varied, surprising, sometimes nonsensical). This is the mechanical basis of "creativity" in AI.

Key Points

  • Temperature is a setting you'll see in most AI tools — now you know what it does
  • It doesn't change what the model "knows" — it changes how it chooses
  • There's no "correct" temperature — it depends on the task (code: low, creative writing: higher)
  • This connects directly to Page 3's probability distributions

Temperature controls how the model chooses. But what it's choosing from is shaped by something you control directly: your prompt.
