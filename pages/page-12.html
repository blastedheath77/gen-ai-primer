In-Context Learning: Teaching by Example

Show, don't just tell

LLMs can pick up a pattern from just a few examples given in the prompt — no retraining needed. Show three examples of translating English to Scots, and it'll attempt a fourth. This is called in-context learning, and it's why LLMs are so versatile.

Key Points

  • In-context learning is emergent — nobody designed it; it falls out of the prediction mechanism
  • This is why LLMs feel "magical" — they generalise from a few examples without retraining
  • The number of examples matters: 0 < 1 < 3, but beyond ~5 returns diminish
  • The model is still just predicting the next token, but examples create a pattern it can extend

Few-shot prompting is powerful. But there's a dark side: sometimes the model generates text that sounds perfect but is completely wrong.
