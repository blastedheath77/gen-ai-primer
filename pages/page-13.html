Hallucination: Confident but Wrong

When prediction goes off the rails — fluently.

An LLM doesn't "know" things — it predicts likely-sounding continuations. It can generate text that is fluent, confident, and completely false. This isn't a bug to patch — it's an inherent consequence of optimising for plausibility, not truth.

Key concepts

Optimises for likelihood plausibility, not truth
Confidence ≠ accuracy tone reveals nothing about truth
Fabricated references real-looking sources that don't exist
Not a bug to simply fix structural — can be reduced, not eliminated
