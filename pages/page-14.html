Alignment: Steering the Model

Teaching AI to be helpful

A raw LLM trained only on prediction can generate toxic, unhelpful, or dangerous content — it just produces what's statistically likely. Alignment steers the model toward helpful, honest, and harmless behaviour. The key technique: humans rate model outputs, and the model learns to prefer highly-rated responses.

Key Points

  • RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback) is the dominant alignment technique
  • The learner just performed the human side of RLHF
  • Alignment is an ongoing process, not a one-time fix
  • Over-alignment is a real problem: models that refuse too much are less useful
  • Alignment shifts probabilities toward better behaviour, doesn't make models infallible
